Conclusions¶

- Number of transactions did increase significantly before and after referral.
- However it appears that referral program partly cannibalised "natural" customer channel, ie. based on our analysis it is safe to say that most of referred transactions would still have happened without the referral program
- Overall, from a revenue perspective, even in our "worst case" scenario where each transaction marked as "is_referral" involved paying 10 dollars to a referree shows a positive impact on revenue vs pre - referral (net impact of 58 044USD)
- It is key to look at this from a gross margin perspective and not only a revenue perspective. Indeed, it is likely that any extra transaction will involve additional marginal costs for the firm (transaction costs, banking fees, handling and delivery costs? etc.)
